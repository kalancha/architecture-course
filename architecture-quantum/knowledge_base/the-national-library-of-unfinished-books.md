# The National Library of Unfinished Books

- Source: https://halupedia.com/the-national-library-of-unfinished-books
- Fetched: 2026-05-23T16:32:31Z

The [National Library of Unfinished Books](https://halupedia.com/the-national-library-of-unfinished-books), located in the submerged city of [Aethelgard](https://halupedia.com/aethelgard), is a specialized repository dedicated to the preservation and study of literary works that were never completed. Founded in 788 AE (After Epoch) by the [Order of the Perpetual Draft](https://halupedia.com/order-of-the-perpetual-draft), the library's mission is to ensure that no narrative, however nascent, is lost to the void of uncreation. The library's collection comprises over 3.7 million manuscripts, scrolls, and digital fragments, representing an estimated 92% of all literary projects initiated globally since the Great Stasis of 412 BE (Before Epoch).

## Collection and Acquisition

Acquisition of new works is managed by a complex system of [Bibliographic Auditors](https://halupedia.com/bibliographic-auditors), who monitor nascent creative impulses through a network of [Empathic Resonators](https://halupedia.com/empathic-resonators). Upon detecting a significant cessation of authorial intent, an Auditor is dispatched to secure the unfinished text. The primary criterion for inclusion is a demonstrable lack of resolution, whether due to authorial abandonment, creative block, accidental destruction of the final pages, or simply a premature cessation of writing. Works are cataloged according to the [Schopenhauer Lexicon of Incompleteness](https://halupedia.com/schopenhauer-lexicon-of-incompleteness), which categorizes unfinished works based on the perceived stage of incompletion, from "Title Page Still Blank" to "Final Sentence Uttered, but Not Transcribed."

Notable items in the collection include the fragment of [Elara Veridian](https://halupedia.com/elara-veridian)'s epic poem, "The Lament of the Luminescent Algae," which breaks off mid-stanza on page 9,871; the sole surviving chapter of [Professor Grumbles](https://halupedia.com/professor-grumbles)' "Treatise on the Aerodynamics of Toast," which abruptly ends with the author reportedly distracted by a rogue crumb; and the entirety of the serialized novel "[The Chronicles of the Interstitial Web](https://halupedia.com/the-chronicles-of-the-interstitial-web)," whose final installment was never penned after the author's sudden ascension to become a sentient [cloud storage entity](https://halupedia.com/cloud-storage-entity).

## Research and Access

The library is a hub for scholars studying [narrative stasis](https://halupedia.com/narrative-stasis), [authorial apathy](https://halupedia.com/authorial-apathy), and the [psychology of the interrupted thought](https://halupedia.com/psychology-of-the-interrupted-thought). The [Institute for Literary Persistence](https://halupedia.com/institute-for-literary-persistence), housed within the library, conducts ongoing research into methods for stimulating narrative completion, though their success rate remains stubbornly low. Access to the collection is highly regulated, requiring researchers to pass a [Certification in Vicarious Completion](https://halupedia.com/certification-in-vicarious-completion) and to sign an affidavit pledging not to attempt to finish any work housed within the library, a practice known as "creative necromancy" and strictly forbidden by the [Edicts of Unmaking](https://halupedia.com/edicts-of-unmaking).

> The tragedy is not that these stories end, but that they never truly began in the eyes of their authors. We are the custodians of potential.
>
> — [Archivist Plum](https://halupedia.com/archivist-plum), Head Librarian of the [National Library of Unfinished Books](https://halupedia.com/the-national-library-of-unfinished-books)

The library's architecture itself reflects its purpose. Designed by the avant-garde firm [Void Construct](https://halupedia.com/void-construct), its many levels descend into the abyssal trenches of [the Abyssal Plain of Lingering Ideas](https://halupedia.com/the-abyssal-plain-of-lingering-ideas). Structural elements often appear to be mid-construction, with scaffolding permanently affixed to facades and staircases leading only to unfinished landings. This design choice, while aesthetically challenging, is intended to create an environment conducive to acknowledging the inherent incompleteness of existence.

> Some libraries seek to contain knowledge. We, however, are dedicated to the quiet dignity of what might have been, the ghosts of stories waiting for a second chance that will, by our very nature, never arrive.
>
> — [Dr. Agnes Parabellum](https://halupedia.com/dr-agnes-parabellum), leading theorist in [Procrastinatory Narratology](https://halupedia.com/procrastinatory-narratology)
