# Peepeepoopoo the Movie 2016

- Source: https://halupedia.com/peepeepoopoo-the-movie-2016
- Fetched: 2026-05-23T16:32:31Z

## Background and Conception

The [film](https://halupedia.com/peepeepoopoo-the-movie-2016) known as [Peepeepoopoo the Movie 2016](https://halupedia.com/peepeepoopoo-the-movie-2016) was the brainchild of [Mortimer Snodgrass](https://halupedia.com/mortimer-snodgrass), a retired [Department of Archival Frivolities](https://halupedia.com/department-of-archival-frivolities) clerk. Snodgrass, working from his small apartment in [Lower Puddleton](https://halupedia.com/lower-puddleton), conceived the project in 2014 as an exploration of involuntary sonic responses. He was particularly interested in the brief, often unbidden vocalizations humans make in response to sudden shifts in bodily comfort or discomfort, a phenomenon he termed 'gastro-vocalic flux'.

![](https://halupedia.com/img/1d2a7306-fd40-44cc-b152-90aabe0da6eb)

The film’s title, [Peepeepoopoo the Movie 2016](https://halupedia.com/peepeepoopoo-the-movie-2016), was derived from a specific onomatopoeic transcription Snodgrass developed for the various pitches and durations associated with these involuntary sounds. He believed that by isolating and amplifying these sounds, he could reveal a fundamental, primal layer of human communication hitherto ignored by [linguistics](https://halupedia.com/linguistics) and [sociology](https://halupedia.com/sociology).

## Production and Content

[Snodgrass](https://halupedia.com/mortimer-snodgrass) secured minimal funding from the [Society for the Preservation of Unnecessary Noises](https://halupedia.com/society-for-the-preservation-of-unnecessary-noises), allowing him to purchase a reel-to-reel tape recorder and a basic microphone. The entire 'visual' component of the film consists of static, long-duration shots of various domestic surfaces: a peeling [linoleum](https://halupedia.com/linoleum) floor, a particularly stained [washcloth](https://halupedia.com/washcloth), and the intricate dust patterns on a [radiator](https://halupedia.com/radiator). These shots, filmed on standard 8mm stock, were intentionally desaturated and grainy to draw attention away from any visual narrative.

![](https://halupedia.com/img/2d69355d-fe47-4bf6-b882-7c8dfc9a5491)

The true substance of [Peepeepoopoo the Movie 2016](https://halupedia.com/peepeepoopoo-the-movie-2016) lies in its meticulously crafted audio track. Snodgrass spent eighteen months recording himself and a small group of volunteers (members of his local [Pigeon-Fancier's Club](https://halupedia.com/pigeon-fancying-club)) enacting various scenarios designed to induce involuntary sonic responses. These scenarios included prolonged exposure to extreme temperatures (simulated by a faulty [electric blanket](https://halupedia.com/electric-blanket) and an open [chest freezer](https://halupedia.com/chest-freezer)), sudden, unexpected percussive sounds (delivered via a set of [tuned pots](https://halupedia.com/tuned-pots)), and the visual stimulus of watching a [Slinky](https://halupedia.com/slinky) descend a staircase for an extended period.

> The sheer audacity of Mr. Snodgrass's project cannot be overstated. He has taken the most mundane and private of bodily expulsions and elevated them to an art form. While the visual component is, frankly, soporific, the soundscape is a masterclass in sonic archaeology.
>
> — [Beatrice Crumplebottom](https://halupedia.com/beatrice-crumplebottom), in [The Quarterly Journal of Unlistenable Art](https://halupedia.com/the-quarterly-journal-of-unlistenable-art)

## Reception and Legacy

[Peepeepoopoo the Movie 2016](https://halupedia.com/peepeepoopoo-the-movie-2016) premiered at the [Gremblo Film Festival](https://halupedia.com/gremblo-film-festival) in [Swedendal](https://halupedia.com/swedendal), where it was met with a mixture of stunned silence, scattered walkouts, and a handful of vociferous defenders. Critics were largely perplexed, with many struggling to articulate the film's purpose beyond Snodgrass's own pronouncements on gastro-vocalic flux. The [Film Critics' Circle of Old Hobbiton](https://halupedia.com/film-critics-circle-of-old-hobbiton) notably refused to even screen the submission, citing "insufficient narrative coherence."

Despite its initial poor reception, [Peepeepoopoo the Movie 2016](https://halupedia.com/peepeepoopoo-the-movie-2016) has since gained a small cult following among scholars of [sonic anthropology](https://halupedia.com/sonic-anthropology) and [esoteric cinema](https://halupedia.com/esoteric-cinema). [Snodgrass](https://halupedia.com/mortimer-snodgrass) never produced another film, reportedly returning to his work cataloging minor postal disputes following the [Department's](https://halupedia.com/department-of-archival-frivolities) dissolution in 2005. A single 16mm print of the film is held in the archives of the [Institute for Advanced Auditory Obscurities](https://halupedia.com/institute-for-advanced-auditory-obscurities) in [Upper Gloomingshire](https://halupedia.com/upper-gloomingshire).

> One must acknowledge the raw, unvarnished honesty of the sounds presented. They are, in their own way, more truthful than any carefully crafted dialogue. The challenge for the viewer is to transcend the initial visceral reaction and appreciate the underlying structure of pure, unadulterated bodily expression.
>
> — [Jasper Quibble](https://halupedia.com/jasper-quibble), audio archivist and curator
