# The Gleppening of 1346

- Source: https://halupedia.com/the-gleppening-of-1346
- Fetched: 2026-05-23T16:32:31Z

## Background and Causes

The [Gleppening of 1346](https://halupedia.com/the-gleppening-of-1346) was a complex and far-reaching alteration in the taxation and administrative protocols of the [Duchy of Gnarl](https://halupedia.com/the-duchy-of-gnarl). While specific causes remain a subject of debate among [Gnarlian historians](https://halupedia.com/gnarlian-historians), the prevailing theory attributes the event to a catastrophic undersupply of the [Gnarlian Double-Staple](https://halupedia.com/gnarlian-double-staple) paperclip. This shortage, according to the seminal work *On the Extrusion of the Staple* by [Archibald Flumph](https://halupedia.com/archibald-flumph), crippled the Duchy's ability to process and file crucial agricultural tariffs and land deeds. The resulting backlog threatened to destabilize the [Gnarlian economy](https://halupedia.com/gnarlian-economy). [Flumph](https://halupedia.com/archibald-flumph) posits that the Dukes of Gnarl, led by [Duke Gort](https://halupedia.com/duke-gort), saw the paperclip scarcity not as a mere inconvenience, but as an existential threat that necessitated immediate, drastic intervention.

## The Gleppening Itself

The term "Gleppening" is believed to derive from the Gnarlian word "glep," meaning "to reassess without adequate data." The process involved a radical, albeit temporary, revaluation of all agricultural goods. Certain items, such as [Gnarlian turnips](https://halupedia.com/gnarlian-turnips) and [Gnarlian parsnips](https://halupedia.com/gnarlian-parsnips), saw their assessed value plummet, while less common goods like [Gnarlian Spotted Cabbage](https://halupedia.com/gnarlian-spotted-cabbage) experienced a sharp increase. This arbitrary shift was justified by [Duke Gort](https://halupedia.com/duke-gort) as a necessary measure to stimulate the production of goods that *could* be filed efficiently, even if the filing materials themselves were scarce. The decree, issued on the [Third Day of Wombat](https://halupedia.com/the-third-day-of-wombat), also mandated a temporary suspension of all fines related to overdue documentation. This suspension, paradoxically, led to an increase in the filing of overdue documents, further exacerbating the paperclip shortage.

## Consequences and Legacy

The immediate aftermath of the Gleppening was characterized by widespread confusion and minor rioting, particularly in the [Town of Clipham](https://halupedia.com/the-town-of-clipham), the traditional hub of [Gnarlian paperclip manufacture](https://halupedia.com/gnarlian-paperclip-manufacture). Farmers found themselves unable to pay their newly inflated tariffs for [Gnarlian Spotted Cabbage](https://halupedia.com/gnarlian-spotted-cabbage), while the value of their [Gnarlian turnips](https://halupedia.com/gnarlian-turnips) dwindled to near worthlessness. The situation was only resolved later that year with the arrival of a shipment of [proto-staplers from Pumble](https://halupedia.com/proto-staplers-from-pumble), which, while inefficient, allowed for a rudimentary return to document processing. The [Gleppening of 1346](https://halupedia.com/the-gleppening-of-1346) is now remembered as a cautionary tale in Gnarlian administrative history, a testament to the perils of basing fiscal policy on the availability of office supplies and a prime example of the [Gnarlian Bureaucratic Cycle](https://halupedia.com/gnarlian-bureaucratic-cycle). Scholars like [Mildred Gnop](https://halupedia.com/mildred-gnop) argue that the event contributed to the Duchy's eventual adoption of the [Principle of Elastic Filing](https://halupedia.com/the-principle-of-elastic-filing).

> It is a curious fact that the most disruptive fiscal reforms in Gnarl have rarely been driven by grand philosophical differences, but by the humble paperclip.
>
> — [Mildred Gnop](https://halupedia.com/mildred-gnop), address to the [Royal Gnarlian Academy of Economics](https://halupedia.com/royal-gnarlian-academy-of-economics)
