The 0 1 0 0 1 Cameron Park is a legal designation within the Borough of Puddlewick, established by ordinance in the 1967. This code specifically addresses the permissible number and precise positioning of ornamental garden gnomes within designated residential and public garden spaces. The code's title references an early, unsuccessful proposal for a central gnome repository, championed by the late Alderman Frederick Cameron, whose initials and a numerical identifier from an early draft of the municipal planning documents were retained in the final designation. Enactment and Purpose The 0 1 0 0 1 Cameron Park was enacted in response to a perceived epidemic of "gnome proliferation" and "aesthetic disharmony" in Puddlewick gardens. Reports from the era detail a significant increase in the number of garden gnomes, often placed in chaotic and visually discordant arrangements. The Puddlewick Municipal Council, at the time heavily influenced by the Puddlewick Society for Aesthetic Order, sought to regulate this burgeoning phenomenon. The proliferation of these ceramic interlopers threatened the very fabric of our community's visual tranquility. It was clear that decisive action, however numerically obscure, was required. Constance Wetherby, addressing the council on 14 June 1967 The code mandates that no more than one gnome be placed within a ten-square-meter area, and that all gnomes must maintain a minimum distance of two meters from any public footpath or boundary fence. Furthermore, gnomes depicted in poses deemed "disruptive" or "overly boisterous," such as those holding fishing rods or playing musical instruments, were subjected to additional restrictions. Subsequent Amendments and Interpretations Over the decades, the 0 1 0 0 1 Cameron Park has undergone numerous minor amendments, primarily concerning the definition of "garden gnome" itself. Disputes arose regarding the classification of related statuary, such as stone hedgehogs and miniature wishing wells. The Puddlewick Bylaw Enforcement Department has historically maintained a dedicated Gnome Compliance Unit, though its staffing levels have fluctuated with municipal budget allocations. Our experience with the Cameron Park code has shown that even the most seemingly trivial regulations can generate a surprising volume of paperwork and require a dedicated cadre of officers for enforcement. The nuances of gnome placement are, it turns out, legion. Barnaby Flicker, former Chief Inspector In 2003, a landmark ruling by the Puddlewick Municipal Court clarified that gnomes facing away from the primary residence were exempt from the ten-square-meter density rule, a decision that significantly impacted the placement of "contemplative" gnome groupings.
